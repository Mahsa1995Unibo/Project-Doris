KE4H · Project 2 · University of Bologna

Framing the Displaced:
How 1938 Newspapers Described Jewish Refugees

A cross-language discourse analysis using Knowledge Graphs, FrameNet, and Large Language Models — comparing English, Italian, and German historical press.

Abstract

This project investigates how Jewish refugees were narratively framed in three different languages of the multilingual historical Knowledge Graph (mhKG) during the critical year of 1938 — the year of the Évian Conference, Italy's racial laws, and Kristallnacht. Using SPARQL queries against the mhKG, we extracted the semantic roles (Victim, Assailant, Agent) assigned to Jewish entities in English, German, and Italian newspaper articles. We then combined these structured findings with Large Language Models to produce qualitative interpretations, comparing zero-shot, few-shot, and chain-of-thought prompting approaches.

Our core finding is that the same displaced population is consistently assigned the Victim role in English-language sources, while German Nazi-era press assigns roles that frame Jewish people as a source of threat. Italian press sits between these poles, reflecting the country's gradual shift toward antisemitic policy during 1938.

Research Question
Are Jewish people and refugees assigned the Victim or Assailant role differently depending on the language and source of the newspaper — and does this framing shift after Kristallnacht on November 9, 1938?

The Three Languages

EN

English Corpus

American newspapers. Covered the Évian Conference and Kristallnacht. Debated refugee immigration quotas.

DE

German Corpus

Hakenkreuzbanner — Nazi party newspaper from Mannheim (1932–1943). Frames Jewish people as a political and cultural threat.

IT

Italian Corpus

Italian press during the year Italy passed its own racial laws, aligning with Germany but with distinct rhetoric.

Project Dimensions

1

Cross-Language Framing

Do the three languages assign different semantic roles to the same entities?

2

Cross-Document Framing

Do different newspapers covering Kristallnacht agree on who caused the violence?

3

Diachronic Analysis

Does the framing change before vs after November 9, 1938?

4

LLM vs KG vs RAG

Which approach best captures the narrative patterns — structured queries, LLMs alone, or both combined?
